Equipment

To be able to fence safely, adequate safety equipment is needed. Basic equipment that is required for all three disciplines include a chest plate, helmet, and glove.



Equipment Description Image
Mask
  • Makes sure your head is protected
  • Gray padding on the bottom of the mask shows that it is a valid target area and can be touched for points
  • Wire connects the mask to the lamé, registering the mask for touches
Glove
  • Protects your hands and allows you to grip the sword comfortably
  • Records all your competitions: whenever you go to a competition, your equipment is checked to make sure it will protect you completely. Once your equipment has been checked, it will be stamped as a confirmation it has passed
  • After going to a couple of competitions, your gloves will be peppered with colourful stamps
Chestplate
  • One of the most crucial safety components of fencing
  • The base barrier between the sword and your body, so it's important to always wear your chestplate
Lamé
  • Worn by foil and sabre fencers
  • Used to show valid target areas on a fencer's body
  • Wired to a scoring machine using a body cord, which is how it registers contact when touched
